# Visual policy editor in the Vault GUI

The visual policy editor helps you create ACL policies with a guided interface
that generates formatted [HashiCorp Configuration Language (HCL)](https://github.com/hashicorp/hcl/) policy syntax.

When you open the visual policy generator for a plugin, Vault prepopulates the
form with the relevant rule paths for that instance. You can review the provided
paths, add new ones, choose capabilities, and save the result as an ACL policy.

## Before you start

- **You must have Vault version 2.0.0 or later.**
- **You must have the appropriate permissions**:
  - To **create ACL policies**, you must have `create` permission for the [`/sys/policies/acl`](/vault/api-docs/system/policies#create-update-acl-policy) endpoint.
- **To get the most out of the policy generator, create secrets in the target secret engine first.**

## Option 1: Generate a policy from within the plugin

1. Sign in to the Vault GUI.
1. Navigate to a supported plugin.
1. Select a secret to generate a policy for.
1. Click **Generate policy** in the page header. The **Generate policy** action may be inside the **Manage** dropdown.
1. Review the prepopulated paths and select capabilities.
1. Click **Save** or expand **Automation snippets** to copy the generated snippet.

![Screenshot of the full visual policy editor modal prepopulated with default paths for the kv plugin](/img/gui/visual-policy-editor-flyout.png)

## Option 2: Create an ACL policy using the visual policy editor

1. Sign in to the Vault GUI.
1. Open the **Access control** menu. 
1. Select **ACL policies**.
1. Click **Create ACL Policy**.
1. Enter the resource path.
1. Select the desired permissions for that path.
1. Click **Add rule** to define capabilities for another path.
1. Click **Create policy** to save the policy.

![create acl policy with visual policy editor](/img/gui/visual-policy-editor-create-acl.png)
