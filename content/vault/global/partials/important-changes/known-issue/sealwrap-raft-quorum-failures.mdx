In Vault 1.17.0 a change was made to assist in diagnosing deadlocks in the sealwrap code.
The deadlock detector adds overhead, and in extreme cases can prevent the Go scheduler
from scheduling the goroutines needed to maintain Raft quorum.  This would typically be
seen on clusters receiving a high volume of requests to sealwrapped mounts, particularly
after a restart or leadership change, since these result in Vault flushing its internal cache.
Reads of cached sealwrapped values do not require locking.

Even without quorum loss, less extreme cases can still increase request latency and
CPU and memory usage.

Symptoms: logs contain messages that start with:

```text
POTENTIAL DEADLOCK
```

which are followed by stack traces.  These do not necessarily indicate deadlocks are
occurring, just that a goroutine had to wait over 30s to acquire a lock.

#### Recommendation

Upgrade to a fixed version (once available) that disables the deadlock detector by default.  There's no
reason to enable it (by adding the string "sealwrap" to the `detect_deadlocks` HCL configuration)
unless support asks you to do so to diagnose a deadlock issue.
