# Set up alerting - Splunk

You can integrate with Splunk to subscribe events you want to monitor.

## Prerequisites

- [Splunk integration token](https://docs.splunk.com/Documentation/Splunk/9.0.3/Data/UsetheHTTPEventCollector)

## Create a Splunk Connection

1. Select **Settings** from the side navigation. Under the **Integration** menu, click **Splunk**.
   ![Nav splunk](/img/docs/vault-radar/integrations/splunk/splunk-int-1.png)

1. Click the **+ Connection** button.
   ![Click connection](/img/docs/vault-radar/integrations/splunk/splunk-int-2.png)

1. Enter a unique name for the connection.

1. Generate an integration token following the [Splunk docs](https://docs.splunk.com/Documentation/Splunk/9.0.3/Data/UsetheHTTPEventCollector)

1. Copy your integration token and paste it into the token field.

1. Enter the port number (default is `8088`).

1. If you are testing with a free trial, select the **Free Trial** check-box.

1. Enter the base URL of your Splunk account.

1. Click **Test & Save**.
   ![Submit connection](/img/docs/vault-radar/integrations/splunk/splunk-int-3.png)

## Create a Splunk subscription

1. Click the **+ Subscription** button to begin the creation process.
   ![Click subscription](/img/docs/vault-radar/integrations/splunk/splunk-int-4.png)

1. Choose a name for the subscription and the saved filters you would like the subscription to use.

1. Choose the connection for the subscription output.

1. Enter the Splunk index.

1. Enter a Splunk source type.

1. Click **Test & Save**
   ![Submit subscription](/img/docs/vault-radar/integrations/splunk/splunk-int-5.png)

Vault Radar events matching your filters will create events in the specified Splunk index.
