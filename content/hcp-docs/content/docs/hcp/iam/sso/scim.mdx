# SCIM provisioning

This topic provides information about SCIM (System for Cross-domain Identity Management) provisioning on HashiCorp Cloud Platform (HCP). SCIM allows you to automatically manage the lifecycle of your HCP users and groups from your identity provider.

## Introduction

SCIM provisioning enables automated user and group management for your HCP organization. When you enable SCIM, changes you make in your identity provider automatically sync to HCP, eliminating the need for manual user invitations and group management.

SCIM provisioning requires SAML single sign-on (SSO) to be configured with the same identity provider. For more information about SSO, refer to [Single sign-on overview](/hcp/docs/hcp/iam/sso).

## What is SCIM?

The [System for Cross-domain Identity Management (SCIM)](https://scim.cloud/) is an open standard that enables automated user provisioning and deprovisioning between identity providers and service providers. With SCIM enabled on HCP, your identity provider becomes the source of truth for user and group information.

SCIM differs from manual provisioning in the following ways:

- **Automated sync** - Users and groups are automatically created, updated, and removed based on changes in your identity provider.
- **Centralized management** - All user and group lifecycle management happens in your identity provider.
- **Real-time updates** - Changes in your identity provider sync to HCP automatically.

## User and group lifecycle

With SCIM provisioning enabled, your identity provider manages the complete lifecycle of users and groups in HCP. The following changes automatically sync from your identity provider to HCP:

- Create users
- Modify user attributes
- Deactivate or delete users
- Create groups
- Modify group information
- Delete groups
- Add or remove group members

When you make these changes in your identity provider, SCIM automatically applies them to HCP. You do not need to manually invite users or create groups in HCP.

## Prerequisites

Before you enable SCIM provisioning, you must configure SAML SSO for your organization with the same identity provider you plan to use for SCIM. For more information, refer to [Set up SAML SSO](/hcp/docs/hcp/iam/sso/setup/saml).

## Enable SCIM provisioning

You must first enable SCIM provisioning on HCP to obtain the credentials needed for configuration with your identity provider.

### Enable on HCP

Complete the following steps to enable SCIM provisioning on HCP:

1. [Log in to HCP](https://portal.cloud.hashicorp.com/) and go to your organization.
1. Click **Organization settings** and then **SCIM provisioning**.
1. Click **Enable SCIM provisioning**.
1. Click **Generate SCIM token**.
1. Copy and save the token value. You need this token to configure your identity provider.
1. Copy the **Base URL**. You need this URL to configure your identity provider.

Keep the HCP page open. You return to it after configuring your identity provider.

### Configure with your identity provider

After you enable SCIM on HCP, configure your identity provider to sync users and groups to HCP. The configuration steps vary by provider.

<Tab heading="Microsoft Entra ID">

### Configure Microsoft Entra ID

#### Prerequisites

- A Microsoft Entra tenant
- A custom application with SAML SSO enabled for your HCP organization

#### Configuration steps

1. Sign in to the [Microsoft Entra admin center](https://entra.microsoft.com/).
1. In the application side navigation, select **Enterprise applications**.
1. Click **All Applications** and navigate to your custom application for HCP.
1. Click **Provisioning** and select **Get started** or **New configuration**.
1. In the **Admin credentials** section:
   - In the **Tenant URL** field, enter the Base URL you copied from HCP and append `?aadOptscim062020` to the URL. For example: `https://api.cloud.hashicorp.com/scim/v2/organizations/YOUR_ORG_ID?aadOptscim062020`
   - In the **Secret token** field, enter the SCIM token you copied from HCP.
1. Click **Test Connection** to verify the configuration.
1. After the connection is verified, click **Save**.
1. Click **Start provisioning**.
1. To assign users and groups, click **Users and groups** in the left navigation. Once assigned, users and groups are automatically created and updated in HCP through your identity provider.

#### Attribute mappings

Configure the following attribute mappings for users in Microsoft Entra ID. Remove any default mappings that are not listed in this table.

**Users:**

| Microsoft Entra ID attribute | SCIM attribute | Required |
| :--------------------------- | :------------- | :------- |
| userPrincipalName | userName | Yes |
| mail | emails[type eq 'work'].value | Yes |
| givenName | name.givenName | Yes |
| surname | name.familyName | Yes |
| displayName | displayName | No |
| Switch([IsSoftDeleted], , 'False', 'True', 'True', 'False') | active | No |
| Join(' ', [givenName], [surname]) | name.formatted | No |
| mailNickname | externalId | No |

<Note>

The email identifier for each user is derived from the **Email** field under **Contact information** in Microsoft Entra ID. Updates to the **User principal name** field are not reflected in HCP.

</Note>

**Groups:**

| Microsoft Entra ID attribute | SCIM attribute | Required |
| :--------------------------- | :------------- | :------- |
| displayName | displayName | Yes |
| members | members | No |
| externalId | externalId | No |

### Configure Okta

#### Prerequisites

- Your current Okta product must support SCIM provisioning.
- A custom application with SAML SSO enabled for your HCP organization.

#### Configuration steps

1. Sign in to [Okta](https://www.okta.com/).
1. In the **Application** tab, select **Browse App Catalog**.
1. Navigate to your custom application for HCP.
1. Click the **Provisioning** tab and click **Configure API integration**.
1. Select the **Enable API Integration** checkbox.
1. In the **Base URL** field, enter the Base URL you copied from HCP.
1. In the **API Token** field, enter `Bearer ` followed by the SCIM token you copied from HCP. For example: `Bearer your-scim-token-here`
1. Click **Test API Credentials** to verify the configuration.
1. Click **Save**.
1. After the configuration is verified, new settings appear under the **Provisioning** tab.
1. Click **Edit**.
1. Select the **Enable** checkbox for the following options:
   - **Create users**
   - **Update User Attributes**
   - **Deactivate Users**
1. Click **Save**.
1. To assign users and groups, click the **Assignments** tab and select **Assign**. Once assigned, users and groups are automatically created and updated in HCP through your identity provider.

The following attributes are supported for users and groups in Okta:

| name.familyName | name.familyName | Yes |
| emails | emails | Yes |

**Groups:**

| Okta attribute | SCIM attribute | Required |
| :------------- | :------------- | :------- |

## Quotas and limits

To ensure optimal performance and prevent sync issues, HCP enforces the following quotas for SCIM provisioning:

- **Users**: 10,000 maximum
- **Groups**: 5,000 maximum
- **Group members**: 5,000 maximum per group

If your organization requires higher limits, contact [HashiCorp support](https://support.hashicorp.com/).

## SCIM provisioning statuses

When SCIM provisioning is enabled for your organization, HCP displays a **SCIM provisioning** status for each user and group. The following table describes the possible statuses:

| Status | Applies to | Definition |
| :----- | :--------- | :--------- |
| Syncing | Users and groups | SCIM is enabled and the user or group is synchronized with your identity provider |
| Not enabled | Users and groups | SCIM has not been enabled or has been disabled |
| Non-SSO user | Users | User uses email and password or GitHub credentials to access HCP |
| Created via SSO | Users | User was created during sign-in through your SSO provider and is not managed by SCIM |
| Created in HCP | Groups | Group was created manually in HCP |

## Conflict handling

When you enable SCIM provisioning, HCP checks your identity provider directory to determine if users or groups already exist in HCP.

### For users

The lifecycle of SSO-based users are now managed by your identity provider instead of HCP.

Users who are not provisioned through SCIM can still authenticate to your HCP organization. They are not rejected or removed.

### For groups

If a group with the same name exists in both HCP and your identity provider, the identity provider group takes precedence and replaces the existing HCP group. The lifecycle of that group is now managed by your identity provider.

When a group is replaced, all existing HCP group membership is cleared and replaced with the membership from your identity provider group.

## Deactivation and reactivation behavior

When you deactivate or reactivate a user or group in your identity provider, the following behavior occurs in HCP:

**When you deactivate a user or group:**
- The entity is deleted from HCP
- All roles associated with that entity are removed

**When you reactivate a user or group:**
- The entity is recreated in HCP as a new record
- You must reassign any roles for that entity in HCP

## Disable SCIM provisioning

You can disable SCIM provisioning at any time. When you disable SCIM provisioning, users and groups remain in HCP, but their lifecycle management transfers from your identity provider to HCP.

Complete the following steps to disable SCIM provisioning:

1. [Log in to HCP](https://portal.cloud.hashicorp.com/) and go to your organization.
1. Click **Organization settings** and then **SCIM provisioning**.
1. Click **Manage** and then **Disable SCIM provisioning**.
1. Type `DISABLE` to confirm the action.
1. Click **Disable**.

After you disable SCIM provisioning, you can manage users and groups directly in HCP or through your SSO provider.
